1. What is inflation?

Increase in the general level of prices for goods and services over a certain period of time, measured in percentage terms (using the Laspeyres method).

2. How is inflation calculated?

The change in the general price level of goods (food and non-food) and services in the current period compared to the previous period.

For example: if the average price of potatoes was 5 somoni per 1 kg in the previous month and rose to 5.5 somoni per 1 kg in the current month, this represents a 10.0% price increase.

A brief explanation of inflation calculations

1. What is the practice of other countries in calculating inflation?

In all countries worldwide, inflation is measured using a consumer basket of goods and services (using the Laspeyres, Paasche, and Fisher index methods).

2. What is a consumer basket?

This is a set of the minimum basket of goods and services necessary to maintain human health, ensure life, and support activity.

Since 2015, the country's consumer basket has consisted of 546 items, including 112 types of food products, 335 types of non-food goods, and 99 types of paid services.

When calculating the inflation rate, the weight (share) of each item in the consumer basket also plays a key role.

3. What is the relative weight of goods and services, and how is it determined?

The share of household expenditures on the purchase of goods and services, expressed as a percentage.

The specific weight is determined based on household expenditures for the purchase of one unit of a good, product, or service relative to the total volume of these expenditures.

In 2026, for the calculation of the inflation rate, the specific weight of food products accounts for 51.7 percent, non-food goods for 36.0 percent, and paid services for 12.3 percent of the consumer basket.

3. What factors cause an increase in the inflation rate in the country?

Seasonal factors — the depletion of agricultural stocks from the previous season and a decrease in the production volumes of such products;

Supply-side factors — as a result of unfavorable weather conditions and, consequently, a significant reduction in agricultural production;

External factors — price increases for imported goods and products on global and regional markets;

Demand factors — an increase in the population’s demand for goods, products, and services as a result of the growth of their monetary income.

Changes in prices of administratively regulated goods and services electricity, water supply (cold and hot water), waste collection, etc.

Foreign currency factors — instability of the national currency exchange rate against to foreign currencies and its impact on the prices of imported goods and products.

4. Which authorized state body calculates inflation?

In the Republic of Tajikistan, the calculation of the inflation rate is carried out by the Agency on Statistics under the President of the Republic of Tajikistan.

5. Why is stability of the inflation rate necessary?

  • Sustainable economic growth in the long term;
  • Strengthening the confidence of the population, entrepreneurs, and domestic and foreign investors in the national currency;
  • Implementation of long-term projects by entrepreneurs and investors;
  • Reduction of risks and uncertainty, as well as interest rates in the economy.

6. What is inflation targeting?

It is a modern monetary policy framework in which target inflation rates for the upcoming year and the medium term are set and announced in advance, and all monetary policy tools are applied to achieve the established inflation targets.

On the importance of inflation targeting.

1. What is the reason for the shift to inflation targeting?

  • - It helps increase confidence in monetary policy and the central bank;
  • - It reduces the level of dollarization in the economy;
  • - It encourages an increase in both domestic and foreign investment in the economy;
  • - It plays a key role in preventing financial crises and mitigating external shocks.

7. Why is the target inflation rate forecasted (set) for the medium-term period?

The impact of measures taken by central (national) banks through the use of monetary policy instruments to maintain a stable inflation rate does not occur immediately; on average, their effects are realized after 3–6 quarters (from 9 months to 1.5 years).

In other words, a change in the refinancing rate does not immediately affect the price level but takes effect only after a certain period.

8. What are inflation expectations?

Conducting surveys among the population of the country to determine expected changes in the price level for the upcoming period.

Since 2019, the National Bank of Tajikistan has been conducting this survey among the population quarterly to determine inflation expectations.

This process helps increase public confidence in the monetary policy of the National Bank of Tajikistan, improves communication with society, and increases citizens’ economic and financial literacy.
